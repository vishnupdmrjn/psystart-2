Assessment Overview

This assessment focuses on students' understanding of the key theories related to identity exploration in emerging adulthood, including Jeffrey Arnett's framework (love, work, and worldviews), and various career development theories by Ginzberg, Holland, Gottfredson, and Savickas.

Part 1: Short Answer Questions (20 marks)

a. Explain the differences between adolescent and emerging adult romantic relationships based on Arnett’s work. (4 marks)

b. How does the question of "Who do I want as a life partner?" reflect identity exploration in emerging adulthood? (3 marks)

a. According to Arnett, how does the nature of work change from adolescence to emerging adulthood? (4 marks)

b. Explain how Ginzberg’s three-stage theory of career development applies to the decision-making process of an emerging adult choosing a career. (5 marks)

a. Summarize William Perry's theory of cognitive development and how it affects worldview exploration in emerging adulthood. (4 marks)

Part 2: Case Study (20 marks)

In this section, students are given a case study about a fictional emerging adult, Sam, who is navigating love, work, and worldview exploration. Based on the case, answer the following questions:

Case Study: Sam is 23 years old and recently graduated with a degree in biology. During college, Sam explored various relationships, but none lasted long. Sam is currently reconsidering career options, as biology no longer feels satisfying. Sam is also struggling to reconcile the religious beliefs from childhood with new ideas encountered in college.

a. Analyze Sam’s relationships during emerging adulthood and suggest how Sam might answer the question, “What kind of person do I want as a life partner?” Use Arnett’s framework to support your response. (5 marks)

b. Based on Ginzberg’s and Holland’s theories, how should Sam approach career decisions moving forward? What personality traits should Sam consider, and how do they fit into potential career paths? (7 marks)

c. Discuss how Sam’s experiences with conflicting worldviews reflect Perry’s theory of cognitive development. What steps could Sam take to reconcile these beliefs? (5 marks)

d. How might Sam apply Savickas' Career Construction Theory to find meaning in a new vocational path? Explain how personal life narratives can shape vocational identity. (3 marks)
