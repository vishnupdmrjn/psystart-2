Gender identity and sexual identity

Interactive Activity

"Understanding Through Scenarios"

Instructions
Step 1: Identify Gender Identity or Sexual Orientation

Scenario 1: "Kiran was assigned female at birth but identifies as male. He prefers to be called ‘he’ and feels most comfortable expressing himself as a man."
Explanation: This is about gender identity, because Kiran is expressing how he sees himself in terms of gender, which is separate from who he is attracted to.

Scenario 2: "Sai is attracted to both men and women, and has been in relationships with people of different genders."
Explanation: This scenario is about sexual orientation, as it describes who Sai is romantically and sexually attracted to.

Scenario 3: "Aarya doesn’t feel comfortable being classified as strictly male or female. They prefer gender-neutral pronouns like 'they' and 'them.'"
Explanation: This is about gender identity, as Aarya’s internal sense of self doesn’t fit within the traditional categories of male or female.

Scenario 4: "Chrissy identifies as a woman but is romantically attracted to other women."
Explanation: This scenario combines both gender identity (Chrissy identifies as a woman) and sexual orientation (her attraction to other women).

Step 2: Reflection

These scenarios illustrate that gender identity and sexual orientation are two distinct aspects of who we are. Gender identity relates to our internal sense of self (how we perceive our own gender), while sexual orientation describes who we are attracted to romantically or sexually. While these aspects of identity are separate, they may intersect and interact in our lives in meaningful ways.

Assessment Overview

1. Define Gender Identity and Sexual Orientation, and explain how they differ. (3 marks)
Marking Scheme:
2 marks for defining gender identity.
2 marks for defining sexual orientation.
1 mark for explaining the key difference between the two.

2. Describe Sandra Bem’s Gender Schema Theory and provide an example of how gender schemas influence memory. (6 marks)
Marking Scheme:
3 marks for describing the core ideas of Gender Schema Theory.
3 marks for providing a relevant example of how gender schemas affect memory (e.g., remembering men as firefighters).

3. Explain the three stages of Kohlberg’s Cognitive Developmental Theory of Gender. (9 marks)
Marking Scheme:
3 marks for each stage (Gender Identity, Gender Stability, Gender Constancy).
1 mark for naming the stage.
2 marks for describing what happens during that stage.

4. How does Social Learning Theory explain the development of gender roles? Provide an example of how reinforcement or modeling influences behavior. (6 marks)
Marking Scheme:
4 marks for explaining Social Learning Theory in the context of gender roles.
2 marks for providing a clear example of reinforcement or modeling (e.g., parents reinforcing toy preferences).

5. Discuss how sexual orientation has evolved beyond a binary model to include concepts like sexual fluidity. (5 marks)
Marking Scheme:
3 marks for explaining the binary model of sexual orientation (homosexual, heterosexual, bisexual).
2 marks for describing the concept of sexual fluidity and how it challenges traditional models.

6. Provide an example of how sexual identity can be fluid across different dimensions (attractions, identity, behavior). (4 marks)
Marking Scheme:
2 marks for defining sexual fluidity across different dimensions.
2 marks for providing a relevant example (e.g., someone whose attractions change over time).
