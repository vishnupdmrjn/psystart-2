Self concept in emerging adulthood

Interactive Activity

"Mirror of Me" Online Reflection Activity

Objective

To help individuals explore and reflect on their self-concept by evaluating personal values, traits, and roles.

Instruction

Create a Digital "Self-Concept Wheel"

Use an online tool like Google Drawings or an interactive mind-mapping app.

Wheel Sections:
  • Personal Traits:   (e.g., "I am...")
    • Write 3 adjectives or qualities that describe you.

  • Social Roles:   (e.g., "In my family/work/community, I am...")
    • Write 2-3 roles you fulfill.

  • Values:   (e.g., "I believe in...")
    • Write 2-3 core values that guide your decisions.

Self-Reflection Prompts:
  • How do these traits and roles reflect who I am today as an emerging adult?
  • Which value is most important to me, and how does it shape my identity?
  • What aspects of myself am I proud of, and what do I wish to develop further?
Visualize Your Future Self:

Use the same tool to add a smaller, future version of yourself next to the wheel. Reflect on:

  • What traits or roles might evolve as I grow into adulthood?
  • How can I align my values with my future aspirations?

If you choose to reflect on a literary or media example, consider:

  • 1. What specific cultural elements shape the character’s identity?
  • 2. How does the character navigate in-group and out-group dynamics, and how does this influence their personal growth?
  • 3. In what ways do cultural expectations create conflict or support for the character’s development?

Assessment (and keywords for answers) - (2x10 = 20 marks)

  • 1. Which of the following best describes self-concept?

    • a. The overall value one places on themselves
    • b. A belief in one's ability to manage life’s demands
    • c. Cognitive schemas that include traits, values, and personal memories
    • d. A sense of control over forces affecting life

  • 2. Self-esteem refers to:

    • a. The belief in one’s ability to cope with life’s demands
    • b. The overall value or worth one places on themselves
    • c. Confidence in controlling life events
    • d. A sense of mastery over life’s challenges

  • 3. Which of the following statements best illustrates self-efficacy?

    • a. A. "I am a good person."
    • b. "I believe I can control my life outcomes."
    • c. "I believe I can control my life outcomes."
    • d. "There’s no point in trying because forces beyond my control influence my life."

  • 4. What is the locus of control primarily focused on?

    • a. Believing in one's ability to handle conflict
    • b. Confidence in controlling personal actions
    • c. Confidence in controlling life events, not just actions
    • d. Managing emotional responses to situations

  • 5. Which of the following is a characteristic of someone with low mastery?>

    • a. "I believe I am capable of handling any situation."
    • b. "There’s no point in trying to change my situation."
    • c. "I know how to regulate my emotions effectively."
    • d. "I handle challenges confidently, even when life throws curveballs."

  • 6. According to Erikson’s Identity vs. Role Confusion stage, what is critical for emerging adults?>

    • a. Self-efficacy
    • b. Emotional regulation
    • c. Mastery
    • d. Self-esteem

  • 7. In Erikson’s theory, how does emotional regulation help emerging adults during identity exploration?>

    • a. By controlling life events to gain mastery
    • b. By helping them avoid situations of conflict
    • c. By managing stress and uncertainty, leading to clearer identity formation
    • d. By ensuring they achieve success in their chosen careers

  • 8. Which theory emphasizes the role of autonomy, competence, and relatedness in shaping self-concept?>

    • a. Possible Selves Theory
    • b. Self-Determination Theory
    • c. Erikson’s Psychosocial Theory
    • d. Cognitive Behavioral Theory

  • 9. According to Self-Determination Theory, which of the following statements best reflects the need for autonomy?>

    • a. "I feel connected to those around me."
    • b. "I feel in control of the decisions I make in my life."
    • c. "I doubt my ability to succeed in new challenges."
    • d. "I’m confident in my academic and career skills."

  • 10. What is a key feature of Possible Selves Theory?>

    • a. It deals with controlling one’s emotions to manage identity crises
    • b. It describes cognitive schemas consisting of traits and values
    • c. It involves imagining future selves, including both desired and feared outcomes
    • d. It focuses on the importance of social connections during identity development
