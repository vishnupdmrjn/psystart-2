Introduction to SocioEmotional Development and identity exploration in Emerging Adulthood

Interactive Activity

For this interactive activity, students will explore their personal identity through the lens of coming-of-age cinema. First, students will list their top 2-3 favorite coming-of-age movies and provide a brief explanation (1-2 sentences) of why each film resonates with them and reflects their own experiences. Next, they will identify their favorite actors from these movies and describe (1-2 sentences) why they admire these actors, linking their admiration to personal growth or identity exploration.

Assessment Overview

Students will write a reflective essay that examines their personal identity development in the context of emerging adulthood. The essay will ask students to analyze their experiences with identity exploration (in breadth and depth) and commitment, using the dualcycle model of identity formation. Students should connect their personal experiences with key theories discussed in the subtopic, such as Erikson’s stages of development and the role of socioemotional growth.

Instruction

Write a 750–1,000word reflective essay addressing the following prompts:

Essay Prompts

1. Identity Exploration: Describe a time when you explored multiple options (breadth) and then evaluated a commitment (depth). Were you confident or unsure in your decisions?

2. Feedback Loop: Have you committed to something and later reconsidered it? What made you rethink your choice?

3. SocioEmotional Development: How have emotional regulation or personal qualities like self efficacy influenced your commitments?

4. Impact on WellBeing: How has your identity journey affected your well being and self esteem?

Purpose of Assessment

This reflective essay encourages students to apply theoretical concepts about identity development and socioemotional growth to their personal experiences. By reflecting on their own identity exploration, students will deepen their understanding of the dualcycle model and the role of emotional regulation, personal agency, and selfconcept in shaping identity during emerging adulthood.
